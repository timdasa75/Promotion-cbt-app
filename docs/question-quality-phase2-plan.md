## Why This Order
1. Near-duplicate families
   These waste learner practice slots and make the bank feel machine-generated.
2. Definition alignment rewrites
   These are high-confidence faults where the stem and options are testing different shapes of knowledge.
3. Weak framing rewrites
   These preserve valid concepts but reduce trust through generic wrappers and polluted metadata.
4. Non-parallel option rebuilds
   This is the largest writing-quality backlog and should be handled in coherent topic slices.
5. Thin explanation enrichment
   These are valuable, but they are lower-risk to defer once the sharper wording problems are isolated.

## Best First Slice
- Start with `policy_analysis/pol_analysis_methods`
- Why:
  - it dominates the near-duplicate family queue
  - the scenario variants are clustered and easy to review together
  - it should produce visible gains quickly without cross-topic ambiguity

## Follow-up Order
- `leadership_management` near-duplicate families
- `civil_service_admin` definition-alignment items
- `psr` definition-alignment items
- then the broader weak-framing and non-parallel-option backlog by topic density
